Properties of Matter

Properties of matter are characteristics or descriptions of matter. These properties could include melting point, shape, color or the flamability of the object. Properties of matter has 2 main categories: Physical and Chemical Properties.

Physical Properties of Matter

  • Physical Properties are properties of matter that can be observed without changing the chemical composition of the object. Physical properties of matter are classified into Extensive and Intensive Properties.
    • Extensive Properties of Matter are properties that vary with the amount of matter. Examples of these are mass, weight and volume.
    • Intensive Properties of Matter are properties that stays the same despite the amount of matter. Examples of these are density, melting point and boiling point.

  • When the box below shrunk, the volume(extenive property) becomes much smaller but the density(intensive property) stays the same.

Chemical Properties of Matter

  • These are properties that describes the object's chemical composition. They can only be observed by changing the chemical composition of the object. Examples of Chemical Properties are:
  1. Combustibility
  2. Rusting

Summary

Properties of matter describes an object. They could be classified into Physical or Chemical.
